Heatwaves

Heat waves are prolonged periods of unusually high temperatures that can last for several days or weeks. In the Philippines, these events are most common during the dry season, specifically peaking between March and May.

Safety & Preparedness

To prevent heat-related illness, follow these core tips:

  • Avoid outdoor exposure between 10:00 AM and 3:00 PM.
  • Stay hydrated and monitor PAGASA advisories.
  • Wear breathable clothing.
  • Check on elderly or vulnerable neighbors.

Historical Records

  • Highest Air Temperature: 42.2°C in Tuguegarao City, Cagayan (recorded in 1912 and 1969).
  • Highest Heat Index: 58°C in Iba, Zambales (recorded in April 2024).
  • Severe Periods: Most severe heatwave periods include 2016, 2019, 2023, and 2024.
